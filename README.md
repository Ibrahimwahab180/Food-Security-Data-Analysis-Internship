Project Overview

This repository documents my six-week data analysis internship focusing on global food security, specifically investigating the complex relationship between economic indicators (GDP, Income Inequality) and agricultural productivity (Crop Yield) on the prevalence of Undernourishment.

The primary goal of this project was to move beyond simple correlations to identify moderating and mediating factors that either hinder or help the translation of economic growth into improved food security outcomes.

Week 1 & 2: Initial Correlation and Data Integration

Objective: Clean and combine initial datasets (Undernourishment, GDP, Crop Yield) and establish baseline trends.
Finding: A clear, but not perfect, inverse correlation between GDP per capita and Undernourishment was found. While wealthier countries generally have lower hunger rates, the variability suggested other critical factors are at play.

Week 3 & 4: The Role of Economic Stability vs. Growth

Objective: Investigate how economic stability factors (like the GINI Index for inequality) moderate the GDP-hunger relationship.
Finding: We established that Income Inequality (GINI Index) is a powerful moderator. High inequality significantly weakens the positive effect of increasing GDP on reducing hunger. Economic growth in highly unequal societies does not efficiently reach the poorest populations.

Objective: Determine if improvements in agricultural production (Crop Yield) act as a mediator that helps overcome the inequality barrier and directly links economic capability (GDP) to food security.
Finding: The mediation model confirmed that Crop Yield acts as a partial mediator. Economic development (GDP) facilitates higher agricultural yields, which in turn leads to lower undernourishment rates. This suggests that investment in agricultural efficiency is a key leverage point for improving food security, even in unequal societies.
